01 · The Problem

Alarm fatigue is killing attention and patients.

Nurses in a busy ICU get hit with hundreds of alarms every shift. Loose leads, a patient rolling over, thresholds that were set for someone else months ago. Constant noise.

After a while, the beeping becomes background. Staff start tuning it out not because they don't care, but because they have no way to tell which alarm actually matters.

In rural hospitals, this hits differently. Fewer staff, more patients per bed, and no reliable internet to pull in cloud tools. By the time a real deterioration shows up, it looks just like the last 200 false alarms.

False alarm Clinically meaningful
02 · The Solution

A second set of eyes that
never gets tired.

VitalSync sits between the bedside monitor and the clinical team. It watches every patient continuously, learns what normal looks like for each of them, and only sends an alert when something is genuinely wrong.

01 / Filter

Stop the noise before it reaches anyone.

Motion artefact. Loose leads. Threshold crossings that don't mean anything for this particular patient. We catch those before they ever fire.

02 / Predict

Catch deterioration hours before it turns critical.

Trained on 200,000 real ICU admissions. The model picks up multi-variate drift that humans miss sepsis, respiratory decline, cardiac instability.

03 / Deploy

No internet required. No data leaves the ward.

A single $400 mini-PC at the nurse's station runs everything. No cloud dependency. No vendor contract. Just works, even in a basement with no signal.

03 · How it works

Three steps from raw waveform
to actionable alert.

A quiet pipeline running on every bed, every second from the moment vitals come in to the moment a clinician gets something worth acting on.

Step 01 · Ingest

Vitals stream in, every second.

HR, SpO₂, BP, respiratory rate, temperature and ECG pulled from standard bedside monitors via HL7 or serial. No new hardware needed.

Step 02 · Analyse

AI runs 31 features across trend windows.

Under 200ms per patient. The model runs entirely on local hardware no outbound calls, no latency from the cloud, no data leaving the building.

Step 03 · Surface

One alert. The one that matters.

Each notification comes with a NEWS2 score, trend context, and a suggested next step. False positives cut by an order of magnitude.

05 · Features

Everything the ward needs.
Nothing it doesn't.

Live ECG & vitals dashboard

Every bed, every waveform, one screen. Refreshes at 60 fps with no lag, even on a $400 mini-PC.

ML deterioration prediction

Up to six hours of early warning for sepsis, respiratory and cardiac decline before symptoms become obvious.

AI shift handover summaries

Auto-generated at every shift change what happened, who to watch, what's next. Saves 20 minutes of scrambling.

Offline-first edge deployment

No cloud, no internet, no data leaving the building. Works in a basement. Works during a blackout. Just works.

NEWS2 clinical scoring

The validated UK standard early-warning score calculated in real time for every patient, not just the ones flagged.

Works on any screen

Wall display, tablet at the bedside, or a phone in your pocket. The same interface adapts to wherever the team is working.
